Secure referral gateway

Simple, secure and trustworthy.

Keep the referral short and useful. Upload the letter, specialist referral, Mental Health Treatment Plan or other supporting document if it already exists; the form captures the essentials IHL needs to begin intake.

Contact details firstMobile, email and preferred contact method sit at the front of the referral.
Secure uploadReferral letters, MHTPs, plans and reports can be attached for intake review.
Trusted handoverReferrer details are kept focused, so IHL can identify, contact and confirm the referrer.
Clinical details and uploaded documents should stay within the approved secure intake pathway in the live build. Campaign metadata remains separate from patient information.

Patient contact

Core details for intake to call, email or text the patient, guardian or referrer.

For Medicare supported referrals, patient address is needed if it is not already on the attached referral.

Referral basis

A brief clinical context, with room for documents to provide the detail.

A short summary is enough when the referral letter or plan is attached.

Immediate risk or crisis should use emergency or acute-care pathways.

This form is reviewed during business hours and is not an emergency pathway.

Upload files

Send the document that already exists, rather than rebuilding it inside the form.

Referral letter, MHTP, psychiatrist or paediatrician letter, Medicare plan, insurer/workplace document, reports or assessments.

A signed referral, plus any supporting plan, can carry the detailed Medicare and clinical requirements. The live form should use secure file handling so referrers can submit what they already have.

Referrer details

Enough to identify and contact the referrer, with provider fields available where funding requires them.

Live submissions require approved secure form handling, file storage, intake notifications and privacy controls before real patient information is accepted.

Medicare-supported referrals may require a written, signed and dated referral with patient details, symptoms or diagnosis, medications where relevant, services requested and plan status. Uploading the source referral and any supporting plan preserves this without asking the referrer to duplicate the document.
