Clinical fit

Thoughtful intake, matched to the right clinician.

IHL provides clinical psychology care across a broad range of presentations. Referrals are considered through a clinical lens, so the next step is appropriate for the patient and practical for the referrer.

The right match matters. Intake uses the information provided to help identify clinician availability, therapeutic fit, appointment mode and any additional context needed before care begins.

  • Who we support Adults, adolescents, children, couples, parents and families.
  • Referral contexts Anxiety, mood, trauma, relationships, parenting, eating concerns, OCD, ADHD, grief, adjustment and complex comorbidity.
  • Care approaches CBT, DBT, Schema Therapy, EMDR, ACT and other evidence-based approaches matched to clinician expertise.

What to include

Information that helps intake move well.

A concise referral is enough when it includes the clinical details needed to understand fit, urgency and the most appropriate next step.

Patient details Name, date of birth, phone, email and guardian details where relevant.
Reason for referral Presenting concerns, functional impact and what support is being requested.
Urgency and risk Any safeguarding, complexity or urgency information intake should know early.
Funding and documents MHTP, private, NDIS, insurance, WorkCover, DVA or reports if available.
Preferences Clinician, appointment mode, language, gender or scheduling needs where clinically relevant.

After a referral is received, intake reviews the information, clarifies anything missing where needed, and helps direct the patient to an appropriate next step. Communication with referrers occurs where clinically appropriate and with patient consent.
