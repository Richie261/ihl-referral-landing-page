What happens next

Referral intake stays human, structured and fast.

The intake team reviews referral details, checks completeness, considers risk and fit, then helps match the patient to an appropriate clinician or pathway.

01

Referral received

HealthLink, online form, intake email, phone and fax are reconciled into the intake workflow.

02

Intake review

The team reviews reason for referral, urgency, funding, attachments and missing information.

03

Clinician matching

Matching considers age, presentation, modality, risk, funding, preference and capacity.

04

Contact and booking

The patient, guardian or referrer is contacted where appropriate, with booking or next steps confirmed.

Clinical service menu

Referral pathways across the lifespan.

A simple service map for referrers. The intake team can advise on fit, availability and whether IHL is the right pathway.

Adults

Anxiety, mood, trauma, adjustment, relationship stress, identity, burnout, complex and comorbid presentations.

Children and adolescents

Developmental, emotional, behavioural, family and school-related presentations, with parent and guardian involvement where appropriate.

Couples and families

Relationship and family-system work where psychological care benefits from a broader relational frame.

Assessment and neuropsychology

Assessment-informed care and referral pathways where cognitive, learning or diagnostic clarification may be relevant.

Evidence-based modalities

CBT, DBT, Schema Therapy, EMDR, ACT and other modality-informed approaches matched to presentation and clinician expertise.

Funding pathways

Medicare MHTP, private, NDIS, WorkCover/insurance and other pathways can be clarified during intake.
