What to include

Referral details.

01

Patient details

Name, date of birth, phone, email and parent or guardian details where relevant.

02

Reason for referral

A brief summary of presenting concerns, functional impact and what support is being requested.

03

Urgency and risk

Any immediate risk, complexity, safeguarding or urgency concerns that intake should know early.

04

Funding pathway

MHTP, private, NDIS, insurance, WorkCover, DVA or other pathway if known.

05

Documents

Referral letter, Mental Health Treatment Plan, reports or assessment documents where available.

06

Preferences

Preferred clinician, appointment mode, language, gender or scheduling needs if clinically relevant.

Referral pathway

Review, matching and next steps.

01

Submit referral

Submit via HealthLink, online form, email, phone or fax.

02

Intake review

Referral reason, urgency, risk, funding and documents are reviewed.

03

Clinician matching

Matching considers presentation, complexity, age, comorbidities and clinician expertise.

04

Appointment and feedback

Updates to the treating team are provided where clinically appropriate and with client consent.

Clinical scope

Multidisciplinary mental health care across the lifespan.

Adults

Anxiety, mood, trauma, adjustment, burnout, identity, relationships and complex comorbid presentations.

Children and adolescents

Developmental, emotional, behavioural, family and school-related presentations.

Couples and families

Care that benefits from a broader relational or family-system frame.

Assessment

Assessment-informed care where cognitive, learning or diagnostic clarification may be relevant.

Evidence-based modalities

CBT, DBT, Schema Therapy, EMDR, ACT and other approaches matched to clinician expertise.

Funding pathways

Medicare MHTP, private, NDIS, WorkCover, insurance and other pathways can be clarified during intake.
