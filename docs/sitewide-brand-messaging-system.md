## Brand Spine

IHL is a clinical psychology practice that combines warm human connection, thoughtful clinician matching and evidence-based care to support long-term wellbeing.

Everything across the website should reinforce four ideas:

1. Right fit matters.
2. Care is evidence-based and personalised.
3. Support is available online and in person.
4. The experience should feel calm, safe and easy to navigate.

## Primary Positioning

Short version:

`Psychological care matched to you.`

Expanded version:

`IHL provides evidence-based psychological care in Bondi Junction and via secure telehealth, matching each client with a clinician whose expertise, style and availability fit their needs.`

Referrer version:

`Refer into a structured intake pathway that matches patients by presentation, complexity and clinical fit.`

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Right-Fit Clinician Matching

Use this when explaining intake, team search, referrals and first contact.

Core message:

`Good therapy starts with the right fit. IHL helps match clients with a psychologist whose experience, approach and availability suit their needs.`

Support points:

- Fit considers presentation, goals, age, modality, preferences and availability.
- Intake should feel contained and human.
- The team is diverse in clinical focus and therapeutic approach.

### 2. Evidence-Based, Personalised Care

Use this across offerings, treatment pages, concerns pages and clinician bios.

Core message:

`IHL offers evidence-based therapy tailored to the person, their goals and the context around them.`

Support points:

- Modalities include CBT, DBT, ACT, Schema Therapy, EMDR, MiCBT, CFT and related approaches.
- Avoid implying one modality is universally right.
- Keep treatment claims grounded and conservative.

### 3. Online and In-Person Access

Use this across the homepage, offerings, get support and location sections.

Core message:

`Care is available from IHL's Bondi Junction rooms and through secure telehealth.`

Support points:

- In-person rooms are calm, professional and practical.
- Online therapy is positioned as the same standard of care in a flexible format.
- Avoid overpromising speed or appointment availability unless verified.

### 4. Warm, Calm, Clinically Credible

Use this as a tone filter for every page.

Core message:

`The website should feel reassuring without being sentimental, clinical without being cold, and clear without overexplaining.`

Support points:

- Prefer plain language over marketing intensity.
- Use warmth through specificity, not flourish.
- Keep claims careful around outcomes, access and funding.

## Voice Rules

Use:

- Calm, direct, human sentences.
- "psychological care", "therapy", "clinician", "psychologist", "intake team".
- "matched to your needs", "evidence-based", "online and in person", "long-term wellbeing".
- "clients" for public consumer pages.
- "patients" only for referrer-facing content.

Avoid:

- Hype language: "unrivalled", "best", "guaranteed", "results-driven" unless clinically reviewed.
- Speed claims: "get started in minutes", "same-day", "within one business day" unless operationally verified.
- Overly casual copy that weakens clinical trust.
- Public copy that mentions internal systems, HubSpot, Practice Brain, vendor decisions or campaign mechanics.
- Context/vibe words such as "boutique" or "Eastern Suburbs" unless approved for SEO or local-area copy.

## Clinical Claim Guardrails

Claims requiring operational or clinical confirmation:

- Appointment availability.
- Same-day triage.
- One-business-day response.
- "Free" consultations.
- Specific clinician availability.
- Current rebate amounts.
- Program dates and group availability.
- Hours of operation.

Use safer phrasing:

- `Availability varies by clinician, presentation and appointment type.`
- `Reception and intake can advise on current options.`
- `Rebates may be available depending on referral pathway and eligibility.`

## Naming and Language Consistency

Use:

- `Institute for Healthy Living` on first mention.
- `IHL` after first mention.
- `Bondi Junction` for location.
- `online therapy` or `secure telehealth`.
- `Mental Health Treatment Plan` or `MHTP`.

Check and standardise:

- `Institute for Healthy Living` vs `Institute For Healthy Living`.
- `Principal Practitioner` spelling.
- `Practitioners` spelling.
- `Mental Health Care Plan` vs `Mental Health Treatment Plan`.
- `client` vs `patient`.
- `info@`, `contact@` and `intake@` usage by page type.
